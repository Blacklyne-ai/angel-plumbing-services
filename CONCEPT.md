## ARCHETYPE

**Name: The Heritage Islington Plumber — "the family on Goswell Road".**

A 40-year, family-run firm sitting on the Islington/City border at 177 Goswell Road,
in the middle of one of London's densest concentrations of Georgian and Victorian
property. The archetype is not "emergency plumber" and not "national multi-trade" —
it is the *trusted local craftsman a Canonbury family keeps on speed-dial for their
Victorian terrace*. Continuity, fabric-sympathy and quiet competence, not speed-and-badges.

**Why it fits this business:**
- 40 years operating in the same square mile (per the existing site: "well established,
  family run … over 40 years' experience … throughout North London").
- Ltd since 2002 (Companies House 04437729) — verifiable longevity.
- Physically embedded in the period-property market: Barnsbury, Canonbury, Clerkenwell
  Georgian/Victorian stock is their literal neighbourhood.
- Distinct from Pimlico Plumbers (mass-market, red/white/blue vans, speed) and Aspect
  (corporate multi-trade). Those own "fast and everywhere". Nobody owns "the heritage
  family that understands your period building" — that is the white space.

## COLOUR RATIONALE (locked palette)

- **Brand main — Deep heritage green `#1F3A2F`.** The Islington Georgian front-door green.
  Walk Cloudesley Square or Canonbury and the railings, doors and ironwork carry exactly
  this register of deep, slightly desaturated green. It signals trade authority and
  heritage *without* the cliché plumber blue/red (hot/cold tap) that every competitor uses.
- **Surface — Warm cream `#F4EFE6`.** Period-paper / lime-plaster warmth. Never pure white.
  Gives the whole site the feel of a good letterhead rather than a 2016 template.
- **Accent — Aged brass `#A8854C`.** The single saturated accent. Brass is the honest
  material cue of the trade — Victorian taps, exposed copper-and-brass pipework, cistern
  levers — used *restrainedly* (rules, small marks, hairlines, the wordmark dot), never as
  loud fills. It is the heritage tap, not a highlighter.
- **Ink — near-black `#161915`** (a green-shifted charcoal, not pure `#000`) for body text.
- **Why not the generic blue+red plumber palette:** blue/red is the hot/cold mass-market
  signal — it reads "call-centre, 24/7, cheap". This brand's value is the opposite:
  considered, local, heritage-fluent. The palette has to say "conservation area", not
  "emergency hotline".

One brand colour (heritage green) + one warm surface (cream) + one metal accent (brass).
Three values. No five-colour splash.

## TYPOGRAPHY RATIONALE

- **Display / headings — Libre Caslon (Text 400/700 + Display).**
  The brief locked *Migra Display 800*. Migra is a commercial Pangram Pangram typeface with
  no free or redistributable web files — it cannot be legally self-hosted for a client site,
  and a render-blocking unlicensed font would be both a legal and a performance liability.
  **Libre Caslon is the correct substitution and arguably a stronger concept:** Caslon was
  cut by William Caslon in *London in the 1720s* — the definitive English Enlightenment /
  Georgian-era typeface, the one the American Declaration of Independence was set in. For a
  firm whose differentiator is *Georgian and Victorian period property in Islington*, a
  period-authentic English heritage serif is more honest than a contemporary geometric serif.
  It serves the identical heritage-craft register Migra was chosen for: high-contrast,
  architectural, editorial, confident. The 700 weight gives the bold/regular mix in the H1;
  Display carries the very large hero setting.
- **Body / UI — Inter Variable (500/600).** Highly legible at the 16–18px the audience
  (period-property homeowners, often 40–65, and landlords) needs. Neutral, modern, lets the
  Caslon carry all the character.
- **Distinct from the rest of the Blacklyne rotation:** not Fraunces (Passionate Lives /
  PECS), not Editorial New (PhysioLogix / 21 Degrees / MK), not PP Neue Montreal
  (Electricians4London / JMB), not GT Sectra (HomeCert). Caslon is reserved here for the one
  brand in the rotation whose entire story *is* English architectural heritage.
- Negative tracking on large Caslon headlines (-0.02em), tight line-height (1.05);
  generous body line-height (1.7).

## WOW FACTOR — PERIOD PROPERTY PLUMBING SPECIALIST

- **What:** a 4-step Astro-island wizard (vanilla JS, no framework). Postcode (validated
  against the Islington/Clerkenwell/North-London coverage set) → property era (Georgian /
  Victorian / Edwardian / Inter-war / Post-war / Modern) → property type (terrace / mansion
  flat / conversion flat / mews / listed / new-build) → job type (bathroom / kitchen /
  boiler-heating / drain-leak / emergency / other). Step 5 returns: era-specific plumbing
  considerations (real, from research — e.g. Victorian: cast-iron drainage at end of life,
  lead supply pipes, facade-visible work needs listed-building consent), a listed-building
  consent flag where relevant, an estimated project-timeline band per job type, and a
  "Book a site visit" lead-capture CTA. Progress persisted to `localStorage`.
- **Why this business:** Islington/Clerkenwell has central London's highest concentration of
  Georgian + Victorian stock; the audience is design-literate and spends £20k–£50k on
  sympathetic bathroom/kitchen restorations. No competitor has this. It converts the exact
  high-value lead this firm is uniquely qualified for, and it *proves* period-property
  fluency rather than asserting it.
- **Secondary wow — Family History Timeline:** editorial, brass-ruled, 1980s → 2002 Ltd →
  present; the heritage no Pimlico can match.
- **Tertiary — CP12 Reminder Tool:** landlord enters last CP12 date → next-due date (last +
  12 months) calculated → optional email capture for a 30-days-before reminder. Honest
  framing ("free reminder, we won't spam you"). Targets the dense local rental market and
  builds recurring revenue.

## MOTION & DEPTH SYSTEM (v2 overhaul)

The site was rebuilt from a flat, single-plane layout into a layered, interactive
experience while staying fast and accessible.

- **Smooth scroll:** Lenis, with anchor links routed through it.
- **Z-axis depth:** multi-layer parallax hero (terrace illustration + floating glass
  chips at different `data-parallax` rates), gradient-mesh + grain + blueprint
  backgrounds, large faded numeral watermarks behind cards, layered shadows
  (`--shadow-soft` / `--shadow-lift` / `--shadow-float`).
- **3D card tilt:** `data-tilt` cards rotate to the cursor; inner content lifts on Z.
- **Scroll-driven motion:** directional, staggered reveals (`data-reveal`, `data-stagger`)
  via IntersectionObserver; animated counters; scroll-progress bar.
- **Cursor:** bespoke dot + trailing ring (fine-pointer devices only), magnetic buttons.
- **Materials:** glass-morphism chips, brass-gradient display text, animated terrace
  window-glow, curved SVG section dividers.

**Accessibility & robustness:** everything respects `prefers-reduced-motion`; cursor/tilt
are disabled on touch/coarse pointers. Critically, motion is **progressive enhancement** -
reveal elements are only hidden when JS is confirmed active (`html.js`), so the content is
fully visible with no JS, and a load-time failsafe force-reveals anything an observer might
miss. `?nofx` disables the JS-gating for debugging/static capture. Implemented in
`src/scripts/motion.ts` + the motion layer of `src/styles/global.css`.
